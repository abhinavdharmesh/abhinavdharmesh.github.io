Research & Projects

  • Atmospheric Modeling for Cosmic Ray Air Shower Simulations (2024 – Present)
    Conducted comparative study of shower maximum depth (Xmax) in vertical proton showers using CORSIKA 7.7550 with GDAS-derived atmospheric profiles. Simulated 1013 eV showers at Agra, India (DEASA site) and Łódź, Poland using multiple hadronic interaction models (QGSJETII-04, SIBYLL 2.3d, GHEISHA, UrQMD). Demonstrated that atmospheric fit quality dominates Xmax stability, providing critical insights for systematic uncertainty assessment in scintillator-based air shower arrays.
  • Autonomous Cosmic Ray Data Logger & Remote Monitoring System (2025 – Present)
    Designed a fully automated data acquisition and logging system using Arduino and Raspberry Pi 400 to record muon detector outputs and environmental sensors (BMP180, DHT11). Implemented:
    • Real-time data validation, automated CSV logging, and 24/7 autonomous operation
    • Remote headless monitoring via permanent ngrok tunnel for instant access to live data
    • Automated backups to multiple external storage devices for redundancy and long-term data integrity
    Demonstrates hands-on experience in electronics, instrumentation, automation, and lab-grade experimental workflows.
